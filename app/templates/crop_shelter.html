What is Crop Shelter?

Crop shelters are protective structures like polyhouses, green nets, or temporary covers that help protect crops from extreme weather conditions such as: rain, heat, frost, and hail.

  • Improves crop survival rate during storms.
  • Helps regulate sunlight and temperature.
  • Prevents pest and disease spread.

Government Subsidy Info

Under the National Horticulture Mission (NHM), farmers can get up to 50% subsidy for polyhouse installation. Contact your local agriculture officer or Krishi Vigyan Kendra for detailed application process and eligibility.

Types of Crop Shelters

Polyhouse

Best for controlled environment farming. High efficiency and yield.

Shade Net

Protects against sunburn and regulates light for crops like spinach, coriander, etc.

Low Tunnel

Small structures ideal for early stage crop protection and frost control.
